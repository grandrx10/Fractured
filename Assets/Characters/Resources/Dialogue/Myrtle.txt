MyrtleTalkTree

[Myrtle]
This is the Royal Redwood tree, scientifically named the sequoia sempervirens, one of the largest trees to grace the realm! It can live over several eons, as long as it is cared for. This one has seen the Great Tsunami, I think. It is said that the Royal Redwoods has a healing effect on those near it, but in my opinion, you should seek the town physician. Haha.
---
[Myrtle]
It is from the kingdom plantae, of the pinophyta division, pinopsida class and of order pinales. Fun fact, the Royal Redwood is monotypic, meaning it's the only living species in its genus. It possesses an extreme morphology, able to exceed over 115 meters in height, and its trunks can be super wide! Its wood density is relatively low, buoyant enough to stay affloat, which is probably how it can support itself-
---
[Myrtle]
It's growth rate is 1.5-2.5 meters per year under ideal care, during it's juvenile stage. This one is well into its teenage years, like me! This specific species of tree exhibits apical growth, meaning the height growth slows after a while, but the trunk growth continues onwards throughout its entire life. It gets most of its water from coastal fog that washes upon the island every now and then, but I feed it a steady supply just to be sure.
---
[Myrtle]
The bark itself has natural antifungal and anti bacterial properties - its bark is also tough enough to survive low intensity fires that would otherwise decimate other trees. This one is a warrior - it survived the Great Fire a few centuries back. Its root system is relatively shallow, but super laterally extensive, spreading out over 30-40 meters, with adventitious roots that sprout even after taking damage.
